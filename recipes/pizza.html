Pizza recipe

Description:

The Classic Margherita Pizza is a true icon of Italian cuisine. Originating from Naples, its beauty lies in its simplicity. It famously features the colors of the Italian flag: vibrant red tomato sauce, creamy white mozzarella, and fresh green basil.

This recipe focuses on creating a perfect, crisp-yet-chewy crust and balancing those simple, high-quality flavors. It's the perfect pizza for any occasion, loved by both kids and adults, and serves as a fantastic base for adding other toppings if you wish.

Ingredients:

  • For the Pizza Dough:
    • 500g "00" or all-purpose flour
    • 1 tsp active dry yeast
    • 1.5 cups (350ml) warm water
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 tsp sugar (to help activate the yeast)
    • 1.5 tsp salt

  • For the Simple Tomato Sauce:
    • 1 can (400g) high-quality crushed tomatoes (San Marzano are best)
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • A pinch of dried oregano

  • For the Toppings:
    • 250g fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or torn
    • A handful of fresh basil leaves
    • A final drizzle of extra virgin olive oil
    • Optional additions:
      • Grated Parmesan cheese
      • Red pepper flakes

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes foamy. Add the flour, salt, and 2 tbsp of olive oil. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.

  2. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Knead for 8-10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  3. First Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and turn it over to coat. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let it rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  4. Prepare the Sauce: While the dough is rising, mix the crushed tomatoes, minced garlic, 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, pepper, and oregano in a small bowl. Set aside. (No cooking is required for a fresh-tasting sauce).

  5. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually 250°C / 500°F). If you have a pizza stone or steel, place it in the oven to heat up.

  6. Shape the Dough: Once risen, punch down the dough and divide it in two (this recipe makes two pizzas). On a floured surface, gently stretch or roll one piece of dough into a 12-inch (30cm) circle.

  7. Assemble the Pizza: Transfer the shaped dough to a piece of parchment paper or a lightly floured pizza peel. Spread half of the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust.

  8. Add Toppings: Arrange half of the fresh mozzarella slices over the sauce.

  9. Bake the Pizza: Carefully slide the pizza (on the parchment paper, if using) onto the hot pizza stone or a preheated baking sheet. Bake for 8-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly charred.

  10. Garnish and Serve: Remove the pizza from the oven. Top it immediately with fresh basil leaves, a final drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, and a sprinkle of Parmesan (if using). Slice and serve hot.

  11. Repeat: Repeat steps 6-10 for the second pizza.
